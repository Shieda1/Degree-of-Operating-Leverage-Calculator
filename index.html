What is the degree of operating leverage?
The degree of operating leverage (DOL) is a measurement of how much of the firm's perating income changes in response to a change in sales. Simply it supports the analysts n measuring the effect of the cahnge in sales on a company's earnings.
How to calculate degree of operating leverage?
To calculate degree of operating leverage you should use the following formula:
degree of operating leverage = change in operating income / change in sales
Example: In the year 2022 company x's operating income increased by 20% for every 14% change in sales, calculate the degree of operating leverage?
Using the degree of operating leverage formula the answer will be:
degree of operating leverage = change in operating income / change in sales

Why use degree of operating leverage?
Analysts use degree of operating leverage because it helps them determine what the effect of a change in sales will be on the firm's earnings. High degree of operating leverage indicates a high sensitive company's earnings to changes in sales.
